Q: What is the top speed of a PTV?
A: Personal Transportation Vehicles have a maximum speed of 25 mph to comply with federal LSV regulations.

Q: Do I need insurance for a PTV?
A: Yes, in most states you need liability insurance to operate a PTV on public roads.

Q: How long does it take to charge a PTV?
A: Most PTVs take 6-8 hours to charge on 110V, or 3-4 hours on 220V.

Q: What's the difference between a PTV and an LSV?
A: PTV (Personal Transportation Vehicle) and LSV (Low-Speed Vehicle) are often used interchangeably. Both refer to street-legal vehicles with a maximum speed of 25 mph.

Q: Can I drive a PTV on the highway?
A: No, PTVs are limited to roads with speed limits of 35 mph or less.

Q: What maintenance does a PTV require?
A: PTVs require minimal maintenance: battery care, tire inspection, brake checks, and periodic service. No oil changes needed.
