## VEHICLE KNOWLEDGE BASE

### Q&A Pairs for Training

**Q: What is a personal transportation vehicle (PTV)?**
A: A personal transportation vehicle (PTV) is a small, electric vehicle designed for local transportation in neighborhoods, communities, resorts, and campuses. PTVs are street-legal on roads with speed limits of 35 mph or less in most states.

**Q: What is the difference between a PTV and a golf cart?**
A: While similar in appearance, PTVs are street-legal vehicles equipped with safety features required by federal law, including headlights, taillights, turn signals, mirrors, seat belts, and a windshield. Standard golf carts lack these safety features and are not street-legal.

**Q: How fast do PTVs go?**
A: PTVs have a maximum speed of 25 mph, as required by federal regulations for Low-Speed Vehicles (LSVs). This speed limit ensures safety while allowing operation on roads with speed limits up to 35 mph in most states.

**Q: How far can a PTV travel on a single charge?**
A: Depending on the model, PTVs can travel between 40-70 miles on a single charge. Factors affecting range include terrain, passenger load, temperature, and driving conditions.

**Q: How long does it take to charge a PTV?**
A: Most PTVs take 6-8 hours to fully charge using a standard 110V outlet, or 3-4 hours with a 220V outlet.

**Q: Do I need a driver's license to operate a PTV?**
A: Yes, a valid driver's license is required to operate a PTV on public roads in all 50 states.

**Q: Do I need to register and insure my PTV?**
A: Yes, in most states PTVs must be registered with the DMV and carry liability insurance, similar to other motor vehicles. Requirements vary by state.

**Q: What maintenance do PTVs require?**
A: PTVs require minimal maintenance compared to gas vehicles. Regular maintenance includes battery care, tire inspection, brake checks, and periodic service intervals. No oil changes are needed.
